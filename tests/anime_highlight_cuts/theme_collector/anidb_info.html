"With my luck there will be no rom in my rom-com..." — Hachiman

The introverted high school student named Hikigaya Hachiman has a very pessimistic view on life, even though he is ranked third in his grade. Not that he has much else to make him an optimist: no friends, no girlfriend, and when people ask him about his future dreams, he replies "out of order".

The modern literature teacher and guidance counsellor, Hiratsuka Shizuka, appalled by Hachiman's outlook on life, gets him to join the Volunteers Club which happens to be run by one of the school's best known and intelligent girls, Yukinoshita Yukino, also known as The Ice Queen. She is driven to excel and believes it is the duty of the exceptional to help the lost lambs of the world.

So will Hachiman be one of the lost lambs that Yukino helps, or will his teenage love comedy turn out just as he expected: SNAFU?

show review
by Zaku88 on 2013-08-26 06:35
Rating: 6.66
Approval: 23.6% (1 votes)
As I expected, Yahari is not your typical love comedy. Source: me. Gone are the pointless, over-the-top ecchi references. Gone are the hopelessly fragile male leads that put up a nice front. Gone are the heroines that are on autopilot to fall in love with the protagonist. In their stead we find characters who can actually speak their mind and are proud to be who they are, regardless of what the rest of the world thinks. Yes, the protagonist does invoke similar reactions from girls as Kodaka from Haganai and yes there is a club for social misfits but the similarities pretty much end there. While there are romantic comedy elements, this is by no means a harem. And while there is some occasional comedy, the show is much more serious minded than Haganai.

Be warned that the protagonist is a pessimist and, though his logic is often flawed, his mood will significantly impact the overall atmosphere of the show. If you're tired of the typical romantic comedy and want to listen to social/psychological discussions from high school students' perspectives, then this is the show for you. If you're looking for a serious romance or significant plot progression, you'll have to wait for season two if it ever materializes.

Animation:
Main character aside, this is a very nice looking show. The show is up to par with clarity and facial animations. It uses some nifty shading effects at times and has very good looking clouds and backgrounds. While the heroines aren't anything out of this world the show seems to try very hard to make Saika stand out as an androgynous character. Hikki, on the other hand, has the eyes of a "dead fish" that, combined with his "rotten" personality, create a negative, creepy aura that pushes away everyone around him.

There are some sporting events but otherwise there isn't much in terms of action. There's the obligatory girls in a swimsuit section but otherwise there isn't your typical dosage of giveaway fan service.

Sound:
The show opens with a refreshingly melodious OP though the first guitar riff makes me think my cell phone is vibrating. The ED is a light rock piece sung by the VA heroines with a fair melody. BGM is standard fare as I can't seem to pick out anything in particular.

One of the reasons I picked up the show was its VA lineup and they definitely don't disappoint. Saori Hayami is the standout as princess Yukino.

Story:
Yahari explores what happens when you force two completely different introverts into the same setting. Our protagonist, Hikki, is a proud loner who is only noticeable due to his creepy aura. He's forced to join the social service club, which happens to have only one member: Yukino. She also happens to be an introvert, though unlike our protagonist she's remarkably talented and pretty. Her main "flaw" is her lack of tact as she says whatever is on her mind, often in a very blunt fashion. These two are joined by a third girl named Yui and work together to help various students solve their issues.

The actual plot can be easily guessed from reading the episode titles. As one may deduce from seeing "He Turns Back on the Path from Whence He Came" multiple times, the story meanders quite a bit, often returning back to square one. To be honest, the actual plot is pretty frail. While there is a pretty good plot twist in the middle of the show, the amount of rising action is pitiful when compared against something like Oreimo 2. Don't expect much of a conclusion either. The majority of the entertainment value of this show comes squarely from the characters and their unique, and occasionally confrontational, interactions.

Character:
Whereas Hikki is always willing to put himself down to resolve situations, Yukino has a second-to-none attitude. This makes for some very interesting interactions when the rest of the class/school is thrown into the mix. Yahari explores some fairly interesting situations within the social structure of a high school. Thinking back, that was such a protected environment but oh I digress. As with most classes, there's the popular crowd that almost everyone wants to be a part of. Assuming the viewer was not part of said crowd, there is definitely a level of understanding with what Hikki thinks, which many shows do not have. Though I don't always follow Hikki's logic, reasoning out situations until one is satisfied is definitely something that I recall doing.

There are definitely the beginnings of a relationship between Hikki and multiple girls, though none of them are fleshed out in as much detail as you'd expect from a full on romance. Unfortunately, they don't make a lot of progress due to Hikki's annoying insistence on not getting on anyone's good side. However, the unique characters and their interactions by themselves merit a positive score.

Value:
This is a hard one for me. While the show definitely had a unique premise and actually carries it through the whole show, the lack of a conclusion just left me with a weird, unfulfilled aftertaste. This is a fun show, no doubt, and there is indeed rewatch value (especially after learning the twist) but ultimately it just doesn't go anywhere. If you're fine with that, by all means dig in.
